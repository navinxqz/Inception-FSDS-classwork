<b>Introduction to statistics</b>

statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, presenting, and organizing data. It provides tools and techniques for making sense of data and drawing meaningful conclusions from it. Statistics is used in various fields such as business, economics, social sciences, health sciences, and many others to make informed decisions based on data analysis.

<b>What is Data?</b>
Everything is Data. Data is a collection of facts, figures, and information that can be analyzed to gain insights and make informed decisions. It can be in the form of numbers, text, images, or any other type of information that can be collected and analyzed. Data is the foundation of statistics, as it provides the raw material for analysis and interpretation. In today's digital age, data is generated at an unprecedented rate, making it essential to have the tools and techniques to analyze and make sense of it effectively.

statistics has 2 types:
1. Descriptive statistics: This type of statistics focuses on summarizing and describing the main features of a dataset. It includes measures such as mean, median, mode, standard deviation, and range. Descriptive statistics helps to understand the central tendency, variability, and distribution of the data.
[consist of organizing & summarizing the data]

2. Inferential statistics: This type of statistics involves making predictions or inferences about a population based on a sample of data. It includes techniques such as hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, and regression analysis. Inferential statistics allows us to draw conclusions and make generalizations about a larger group based on the information obtained from a smaller subset of data.
[Can form a conclusion based on the data]

Theory used in Descriptive statistics:
1. Measure of central tendency (Mean, mediun, mode)
2. Measure of dispension (Variance, standard deviation)
3. Different type of distribution, plotting, graph

Theory used in Inferential statistics:
1. Z-test
2. t-test
3. Anova test
4. chi-square test
they are all hypothesis testing (p value). We can get a conclusion based on those measurements.
